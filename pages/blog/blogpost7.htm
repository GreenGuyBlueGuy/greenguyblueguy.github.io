Global Game Jam 2025

By Joe Little • January 2025

Participating in Global Game Jam 2025 was an exciting and memorable experience. The theme, “Bubbles,” encouraged rapid creative exploration and collaboration with other developers.

The event was hosted by the Greater Gaming Society in San Antonio at the UTSA campus. After a slightly chaotic arrival involving parking logistics and a long walk to the venue, the team arrived just in time for the theme reveal.

Once the theme was announced, brainstorming began immediately. Shortly after, additional developers joined the team, expanding the scope of ideas and collaboration. The group explored multiple interpretations of “Bubbles,” eventually developing a multi-layered concept.

The final design incorporated three perspectives: a literal bubble protagonist, a narrative system where the player challenges an authoritative narrator, and reflective “thought bubble” segments featuring philosophical quotes between stages. This structure added thematic depth and narrative cohesion.

The concept also drew on personal reflection about childhood experiences with bubbles and their transient nature. This inspired a philosophical direction for the game, focusing on impermanence and acceptance of outcomes rather than resistance.

Influences included narrative-driven experiences such as The Stanley Parable and fast-paced arcade titles like Downwell. The development team worked intensively throughout the jam to bring the concept to completion within the deadline.

The result was Bubble Down, submitted successfully for judging. The project received strong feedback during presentations, ultimately earning the “Most Creative” award.
